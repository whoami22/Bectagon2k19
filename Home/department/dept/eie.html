Paper Presentation

Rules

  • Maximum number of participants in a Team is 2.
  • Abstract should not exceed 1 page and Paper should not exceed 15 pages.
  • The subject of the mail with the attached abstract and later, the final paper should be the domain and sub-category under which the paper is categorized.
  • Bring your college I-Card.
  • Please bring your PowerPoint presentation on a CD or Pen-Drive.
  • Please bring 2 hardcopies of the submitted paper on the day of the event.
  • The teams will get 10 minutes for presentation followed by question and answer session.
  • Persons from different institutions can be a part of the same team. However, one person may not be a part of multiple teams for the same event.
  • The mail with submissions should contain:
    • Title- theme of the paper.
    • Name of both the authors .
    • Phone no of both the authors.
    • E- mail ID .
  • Paper must contain index, list of figures, list of tables, abstract, introduction, point wise description of subject and conclusion and references.
  • Paper must be preceded by a cover page specifying the title of the paper, names of authors and their college names as also their contact numbers and email ids.
  • Last day to submit soft copy of your report (via e-mail) will be two days prior to the day of presentation.
  • After you have submitted the soft copy, a panel of judges will go through your material and if you are shortlisted to give the presentation, you will be notified.
  • The abstract mail should contain abstract, references, your e-mail id and mobile number.
  • The decision of judges will be final and no arguments or appeal will be entertained.

Paper Presentation Topics:

The presentation topics, but not limited to, are given below:
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Neural Networks and Fuzzy logic
  • Industrial Automation
  • Nano technology
  • Internet of Things
  • Optical fiber communication
  • Digital image processing
  • Embedded system
  • Smart Sensor Networks
  • 5G mobile technology

Workshop

Rules

  • workshop is on Programmable Logic Controllers (Rexroth-PLCs)
  • It is conducted on two days.
  • The workshop participants are limited. (First come first serve basis)
  • The work shop covers both theory and practical sessions.
  • The theory session is in the morning and practical session is in the afternoon on both days.
  • Certificates are provided for the participants.

Circuitrix

Rules

  • Teams of three participants only can contest for this event.
  • There will be total 3 rounds.
  • Original Schematic diagram and faulty circuit will be given.
  • Team has to locate the faults successfully within time limit.
  • The teams will be promoted to the next round that has completed the task with minimum time.
  • First round will be elimination round with simple circuits from fields of electronics.
  • Difficulty level will be raised as teams moving to the final round
  • All the components and material required for the circuit will be provided by us.
  • Power supply and other testing devices like multimeter, CRO (if needed) and other tools will be made available in a shared hall.
  • Limited entries are accepted.
