General Quiz

DESCRIPTION:

Knowledge is power inde ed. There is nothing more exciting than trying to figure out answer for various questions chosen from various domains of knowledge. So, for all the curious minds out there, this quiz is for you!

TOPICS:

The topics for the quiz are General awareness, Sports, Politics, Indian culture and History, Abbreviations, Monuments, Awards, Books & Author, Film Industry, etc.

RULES:

  • Team must comprise of 3 participants from same college
  • Screening test will be conducted for all the teams attended
  • Selected teams will participate in the final quiz.
  • Quiz consists of three rounds
    • Round1 – Oral Questions
    • Round2 – Visual round
    • Round3 – Rapidfire
  • Mobile phones or any gadgets are not allowed while participating.

JUDGING CRITERIA:

  • Quiz master decision is final
  • Scores from each round are carried to next round
  • The team with highest score will win

Floor Crossing

DESCRIPTION:

Floor crossing is a form of debate where the speaker literally debates against oneself. Speaker starts by taking a stance on the topic and switches sides when judge says cross the floor.

RULES:

  • Topics are given on the spot
  • Each speaker has to speak for 3minutes on the given topic

The order of changing must be as follows:
Speaker can start speaking about the topic in order of supporting or opposing the topic depends on his/her interest. After certain point of time, speaker should follow the instructions of examiner to switch to the opposite of what he/she started about the topic after listening to “cross the floor” by examiner. But the points should be contradicting.

JUDGING CRITERIA:

  • 40% content
  • 20% non-verbal communication-Eye contact, body language and poise
  • 20% voice and articulation-volume, pace, rhythm, intonation and proper pronunciation
  • 20% language -Appropriateness and accurate use of language

EXAMPLE TOPICS:

  • Animal testing
  • Censor in media
  • School uniforms

Stand Up Comedy

DESCRIPTION:

Stand-up comedy is a comic style in which a comedian performs in front of a live audience, usually speaking directly to them. The performer is commonly known as a comic, stand-up comic, comedian, stand-up comedian, or simply a stand-up. In stand-up comedy, the comedian recites a grouping of humorous stories, jokes and one-liners typically called a monologue. Stand-up comedians can use props, music or magic tricks to enhance their acts.

RULES:

  • Duration is of 5 to 10 minutes
  • Should express only in English

JUDGING CRITERIA:

  • Innovative way of expressions
  • Humorous
  • Attracting people and should involve audience too
  • Voice and articulation-volume, pace, rhythm, intonation and proper pronunciation

Dear Diary

DESCRIPTION:

Everyone has stories; they’d rather keep in a locker chest. With the help of a torn entry from the stolen dairy of a stranger, step into that person’s shoes and use your imagination to pen down a tale worth hiding.
We all know half completed stories are more exasperating than even complete stories with lousy endings. While there are people waiting patiently for the ending, there are those creative folk who are willing to put their pen on paper to end it in their own way. This event is exclusively for those who want to put their pen on paper. Participants are given an entry from a dairy of a person and they get to continue that story.

RULES:

  • The participants are given an entry from a dairy with a specified timeline, each participant’s entries being disparate.
  • The participants have to continue the story in the entry and end it.

JUDGING CRITERIA:

    The entries are judged on:
  • Grammar
  • Relevance of the story to the given prompt
  • Vocabulary
  • Consistency with the given timeline
